Thank you for trying out my weather app!

This was coded from the ground up, starting with a blank white screen. This is a 100% frontend project as this does not connect to any backend. All forecasts are randomized, data grouped by season (summer, spring, fall, winter).

App Components

This is what you're currently looking at. To the right of "App Components", you'll find the "+/-" button which'll toggle all sections. In other words, all collapsed sections will become expanded & all expanded will collapse. I know there's a lot of information here, so I hope the accordian interaction helps with usability and visual attraction. If you'd like to view the source code, feel free to visit my github repository.
This is the first screen you see on page load. In the top-left corner, resides an info button to view all details about this application. Each form field (street, city, state, country, zip) is required. Some fields such as street, city, and zip have additional validation checks. No worries though. If a field value isn't considered valid, the first invalid field will autofocus and a tip will appear directly below.

If you include a season in the street name, that'll influence the forecast you receive. If you don't, your forecast's season will be randomized. Here's some examples to try:

1234 Summer Cv.
2345 Spring Mdw.
3456 Fall Flt.
4567 Winter Vly.
5678 Random Blvd.
This is seen when the "State: " dropdown is selected. You'll find all 50 states in alphabetical order, preceeded by its abbreviation.

Once a state is selected, country will auto-populate to "US - United States" and become disabled since all states are within the US. This is to help save time / avoid mismatching between state and country. If "N/A" is chosen for state value, country is reset and enabled for selection.

This is seen when "Country: " dropdown is selected. You'll find a vast majority of countries from around the world in alphabetical order, preceeded by its abbreviation.
Assuming all form fields are valid, this is the screen that appears after clicking "Ask Mother Nature" button at the bottom of the form. In the top-left corner, resides a back button to make any edits to your form fields. Its content displays the next 7 days' weather (hypothetically - all data is randomized). The form's street value will determine the generated forecast and background color. You can find a breakdown of how each season affects the forecast's temperatures, weather effect, & background color in the "Temp-Weather" section below.
If the street value does not contain a season, one will be chosen randomly, each with a 25% chance.
All low temperatures will be 5 - 15 degrees colder than their corresponding high temperature.
